## Notes

### Urban vs Rural Differences

| Sound | Urban | Rural | Example |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|
| ق (qaf) | ' (glottal stop) | q | قال → 'aal (urban) / qaal (rural) |
| ث (tha) | s | s | ثلاثة → tlaate |
| ذ (dhal) | z | z | ذهب → zahab |
| ظ (dha) | z | z | ظرف → zarf |

### Emphatic Consonants

The following consonants are emphatic and cause neighboring vowels to be emphasized:
- S (ص) - emphatic s
- D (ض) - emphatic d
- T (ط) - emphatic t
- z (ظ) - emphatic z (in Levantine, merged with z)

### Glottal Stop (')

The glottal stop ' is represented by:
- أ (alif with hamza above)
- إ (alif with hamza below)
- ء (hamza alone)
- ئ (ya with hamza)
- ؤ (waw with hamza)
- ق (qaf) in urban dialects
